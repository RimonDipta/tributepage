ELON MUSK
"Any product that needs a manual to work is broken."

Quick Information:

Elon Reeve Musk FRS is a business magnate and investor. He is the founder, CEO and chief engineer of SpaceX; angel investor, CEO and product architect of Tesla, Inc.; founder of The Boring Company; co-founder of Neuralink and OpenAI; president of the Musk Foundation; and owner and CEO of Twitter, Inc. With an estimated net worth of around $181 billion as of November 18, 2022, Musk is the wealthiest person in the world, according to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index and Forbes's real-time billionaires list.

Timeline of few remarkable events

  • 1971: Born to an engineer father and model mother
  • 1983: Writes video game called Blastar, sells it for $500
  • 1994: Obtains Wharton Degree (in Economics and Physics)
  • 1995: Leaves Stanford PHD after 2 days
  • 1995: Starts Zip2 with brother, coding it himself
  • 1996: Raised $3M in venture capital (and is later kicked from CEO to CTO role by investors)
  • 1999: Gets $22M from sale of Zip2
  • 1999: Starts X.com, an online bank
  • 2000: Merges X.com with Confinity (a competing startup) forming PayPal
  • 2000: Gets ousted as CEO from Paypal
  • 2001: Takes trip to Russia to buy rocket to send to Mars
  • 2002: Starts SpaceX (with own money)
  • 2002: Gets $250M from PayPal sale
  • 2004: Meets Tesla founders and decides to invest into the company
  • 2006: First ever SpaceX rocket launches and explodes
  • 2006: Wins NASA contracts to demo cargo delivery to ISS and make Falcon-9
  • 2006: Becomes first investor in cousin's company SolarCity
  • 2007: Second launch fails
  • 2008: First Tesla customers buy their Roadsters
  • 2008: Third launch fails destroying 4 satellites aboard including NASA's
  • 2008: Wins NASA conract for 12 flights for $1.6B
  • 2008: Fourth launch succeeds
  • 2008: Nearly runs out of cash for Tesla and SpaceX. Hours before Tesla would have gone bankrupt, funding is raised
  • 2009: Unveils Tesla Model S
  • 2010: Buys car factory for cheap as a result of Toyota's failed joint venture
  • 2010: Tesla officially goes public ending the day of IPO at $24/share
  • 2011: Makes bold promise to send humans to Mars by 2021-2031. Provides plan for reusable rockets to make cost of flights 100 times cheaper.
  • 2012: Unveils Tesla's SUV: Model X
  • 2012: SpaceX Dragon docks with the International Space Station
  • 2012: Tesla starts shipping Model S to customers
  • 2012: Launches supercharger network (for long distance Tesla driving)
  • 2013: Publishes design of Hyperloop high-speed rail
  • 2014: Starts building Gigafactory to mass produce batteries for Tesla vehicles
  • 2015: First customers get their Model X
  • 2015: Manages to successfully land reusable rocket after reaching orbit
